Inkblot: The Undergraduate Journal of Psychology

The University of Toronto

Inkblot is an Undergraduate Journal of Psychology written and put together by students at the University of Toronto. Articles are submitted by undergraduate students, which then go through several rounds of editing and review by the editorial team made up of undergraduate psychology students, selected and interviewed by the Editor-in-Chief. Several psychology professors act as faculty advisors.

The journal features a wide range of psychology papers, from literature reviews to critiques to original research. This journal is published annually, in electronic format in May and in print format in September.

Inkblot has 4 main goals:

  1. To showcase exemplary undergraduate writing
  2. To provide a medium for enthusiastic individuals to demonstrate their creativity
  3. To generate further interest in psychology through accessible and interesting articles
  4. To provide a platform for students to share their opinions, ideas, and experiences in improving our own mental health, starting with the lives that we can touch in the community of our university
